Research

Past research

Automatic isochrone fitting

I studied how to automatically fit isochrones to a star cluster in a colour-magnitude diagram.

Isochorones are theoretical curves used to estimate a stellar population age, distance, reddening and metalicity.

Although very reliable, these curves are mainly fitted manually. This makes impratical to study thousands clusters, not to mention author divergence.

Using parallelism, I made a CPU-bound algorithm that outruns existing MCMC routines.

Milky Way substructures

I studied the relationship between open star clusters with MW's substructures, such as stellar streams.

Open clusters are located in the Milky Way's disk. In fact, they constitute our Galaxy spiral arms.

With recent census, data showed that some open clusters are located in the thin disk and also in the halo.

We studied how these clusters ended up in this regions of Galaxy and tried to identify possible links between the clusters orbit and merger or flyby events.

Clustering algorithms applied to stellar populations

I applied unsupervised clustering algorithms to identify and charcterize open star clusters.

With the GAIA revolution, many stellar clusters were discovered and many more are yet to be discovered.

However, the star-members list between authors can present divergence as high as 20%.

Using unsupervised clustering algorithms, I studied how to automate the process of identify and characterize open star clusters.
